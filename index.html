Sputnik 1 (1957)

Launch Date: October 4, 1957

Country: Soviet Union (USSR)

Sputnik 1 was the world's first artificial satellite. It orbited Earth every 96 minutes and transmitted radio signals back to Earth, proving that objects could be placed in orbit.

This event marked the beginning of the Space Age and triggered the space race between the USSR and the USA.

Yuri Gagarin (1961)

Mission: Vostok 1

Date: April 12, 1961

Country: Soviet Union (USSR)

Yuri Gagarin became the first human to travel into space and orbit the Earth. His spacecraft, Vostok 1, completed one full orbit in just 108 minutes.

This historic achievement proved that humans could survive and function in space, marking a major victory for the Soviet Union during the Space Race.

His famous phrase during the mission: "Poyekhali!" ("Let's go!")

Apollo 11 (1969)

Mission Date: July 20, 1969

Astronauts: Neil Armstrong, Buzz Aldrin, Michael Collins

Country: United States (NASA)

Apollo 11 was the first mission to land humans on the Moon. Neil Armstrong became the first person to step onto the lunar surface, followed by Buzz Aldrin, while Michael Collins remained in orbit.

Armstrong’s famous words: "That's one small step for man, one giant leap for mankind."

This mission marked a major victory in the Space Race and remains one of the greatest achievements in human history.

Space Shuttle Columbia (1981)

Mission: STS-1

Date: April 12, 1981

Crew: John Young, Robert Crippen

Country: United States (NASA)

Space Shuttle Columbia marked the first flight of NASA’s Space Shuttle program and the beginning of the reusable spacecraft era.

Unlike previous missions, the shuttle could be launched into orbit and then return to Earth to be reused, significantly changing the economics and possibilities of space travel.

This mission demonstrated a new era of human spaceflight, paving the way for satellite deployment, scientific research, and construction of the ISS.

International Space Station (1998–Present)

First Module Launch: November 20, 1998

Orbit: Low Earth Orbit (~400 km above Earth)

Partners: NASA, Roscosmos, ESA, JAXA, CSA

The International Space Station (ISS) is the largest human-made structure in space and serves as a microgravity research laboratory.

It represents one of the greatest examples of international cooperation, with astronauts from around the world living and working together in orbit.

Since 2000, the ISS has maintained a continuous human presence, contributing to scientific research, Earth observation, and future deep-space missions.

Artemis & Commercial Space Era (2020s)

Era: 2020s–Present

Programs: Artemis, SpaceX, Blue Origin

Goal: Return humans to the Moon and prepare for Mars missions

NASA’s Artemis program aims to return humans to the Moon, including the first woman and the next man, establishing a sustainable lunar presence.

At the same time, private companies like SpaceX are revolutionizing spaceflight with reusable rockets and commercial crew missions.

This era marks the transition from government-led exploration to a collaborative model between space agencies and private industry.
